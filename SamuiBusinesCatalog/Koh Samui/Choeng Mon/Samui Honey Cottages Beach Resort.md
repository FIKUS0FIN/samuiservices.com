- **Glasgow girl** (2/5): "This place has the potential to be great with some updating and more care for residents. I think the previous reviews are very flattering as we did not enjoy our stay. Rooms are very dated and bathrooms terrible, poor shower and you can’t flush paper. Shabby curtains, bedding etc. It has a small pool next to the restaurant, although small its quite nice and modern. It only has 7 loungers at the pool so you will be lucky to get one. Outside folk can use the facilities all day as long as they spend 250 Baht so you will find randoms using the loungers and in the pool, leaving no room for residents. There are loungers on the beach but they are filthy and not set up each day. There is very loud music at pool/ restaurant that plays all day. Owner sits about the bar/ restaurant playing loud videos on his phone. Felt dirty here the whole time as there are no proper paths round the resort and the sand just gets everywhere. My feet were black walking in the room with no shoes. Positives - it’s a nice setting and you get lots of bottled water each day in your room,  restaurant Kirati next door is good. Happy hour BOGOF  for certain cocktails is ok and they left a valentine gift in our room which was a nice touch I could not wait to leave and would not stay here again."
- **Joni Leinonen** (2/5): "Paid only 60 € per night but even that was too much. The room above the reception was very dated and dirty. Brownish stains all over the walls, layers of dust in places that would be extremely easy to clean etc. Plus the huge cockroach I had to evict from the room. The pool and the common areas were in a somewhat better condition apart from the plastic sunbeads that had seen better days. Also the smell of seweage in the reception was pretty nasty.  However, the bed in the room was the worst part of the experience. The matress was extremely bouncy and you could feel every spring. I woke up every time my partner moved even an inch. Impossible to get a good night’s sleep.  The staff at the reception weren’t exactly rude but didn’t say hello, didn’t ask how our stay was, didn’t offer to order a taxi to the airport, didn’t help with our luggage etc. I don’t necessarily need all that but in every other hotel in Thailand the staff at least bothered to say hello when passing by.  Couple of positives; the location on the beach was good, and the breakfast was decent. The pool itself was also okay."
