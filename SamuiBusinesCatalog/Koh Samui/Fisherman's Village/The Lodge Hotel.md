### Top Reviews
- **Shay Nissel** (5/5): "We were pleasantly surprised by this hotel! Despite the low price point during the busy season, it delivered in a big way.  Pros: Fantastic location near shops and restaurants. The room literally opens onto the sand. The staff is top-tier; they were very helpful with our luggage and airport transport. Great extras like beach gear and mosquito screens.  Cons: Our ground-floor room was close to the street, so it can be noisy at night.  Maintenance Note: The bathroom looks updated, but there was a significant water leak from the shower that flooded the floor.  Overall, it’s a wonderful spot if you want to be steps from the ocean, though I’d recommend an upper floor if you are sensitive to street noise."
- **Zuzana Zajacová** (2/5): "I am rating only food, service and beach. This beach is OK, there are more better beaches at Koh Samui, but for Fisherman village is a good spot couse you can find some shadow there if you wanna use beach during the day. We were not a hotel guests we just came for beach. They let us sit at the chairs on the beach couse sunbeds are only for hotel guests. Service I will rate only 1* couse no one came to ask what we want, I had to go and request the menu also after that I had to go to restaurant to order food and no one came to clean the table after and we were sitting there for hours. I had fried rice with beef, beef was very hard, not recommend this dish. I didn't like the food much and I think it was quite pricy for Thailand to pay for fried rice which was not a good quality. I dont remember 100% but I think it was 280baht."
- **Alan Berlin** (1/5): "Our review relates exclusively to the communication and the handling of our cancellation.  We had booked a stay from March 5 to March 7 and contacted the property 19 days before arrival because we were unsure whether we would be able to travel.  We were informed that a refund would only be possible if we could provide documentation proving that we were unable to travel. At the same time, it was mentioned that the situation could be reviewed if the room were rebooked. Based on this, we cancelled the booking so the room could be listed again.  After our cancellation, it became clearly visible online that there was no longer any availability for the period from March 5 to March 7, which strongly suggested that the room had been booked again. Nevertheless, a refund was refused with reference to the original cancellation policy.  While this may formally comply with the booking terms, the way this situation was handled is very disappointing from our perspective. We cooperated and cancelled early so the room could be offered again, yet we are the only ones left with the financial disadvantage.  In a tourism-driven destination like this, we would have expected considerably more goodwill and fairness in dealing with guests in such a situation."
