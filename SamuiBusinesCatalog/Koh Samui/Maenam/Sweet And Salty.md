### Top Reviews

- **V A** (3/5): "Overall, a well-run café with solid quality — but not one we’d go out of the way for, given the many options around Koh Samui.  - We dropped by during a slightly busy hour, but the staff were kind enough to slot us in with a time cap, which was appreciated - The raf coffee was excellent — rich, creamy, and one of the more reliable Russian-style coffees we’ve had across cafés - We also tried a watermelon juice (nice, refreshing) and the orange espresso, which had a bold, pulpy orange base that complemented the coffee well — raw, sharp, and quite enjoyable  - For food, we ordered the Draniki (Russian potato pancakes), which came as two thick stacks with veggies and salmon — the pancakes were hearty, but the salmon tasted a bit too strong or gamey for our liking - That said, it might just be the traditional prep — different from the smoked salmon we’re used to in places like Dubai, Singapore, or Switzerland - The big breakfast plate was a variation on the same theme — with eggs, bread, and the same pancakes; the scrambled eggs were fine, bread was unremarkable  ✅ Overall, we appreciated the effort and authenticity, but it didn’t fully land for our taste — probably more suited to those familiar with or craving Eastern European flavours"
