- **Alessandro Xine** (3/5): "I stayed at Tolani Hotel Samui with fairly high expectations, but unfortunately the experience didn’t quite match up.  The room I was given felt quite worn and “lived in” not in a charming way, but more like it hadn’t been properly refreshed in a long time. You could see signs of aging in several details, and overall it didn’t give that clean, well maintained feeling you’d expect at this level.  What was more surprising is that the hotel was practically empty during my stay, yet no upgrade or even small perks were offered. This is where the guest relations really fell short. Even basic gestures that make a guest feel welcomed or valued were missing, which made the experience feel a bit cold and transactional.  The property itself is huge, which on paper sounds great, but in reality it can feel a bit too spread out and lacking atmosphere when occupancy is low. It almost gives a slightly abandoned vibe in certain areas, especially during quieter times of the day.  The beach is another mixed point it’s wide and scenic, but during low tide the water goes very far out, making it difficult to actually swim or enjoy the sea for a good part of the day.  In terms of location, it’s quite far from the main hotspots in Samui. If you’re looking to be near the action like Chaweng or even the more relaxed Fisherman’s Village in Bophut, this isn’t ideal. You’ll definitely need a scooter or car to move around comfortably, otherwise you might feel a bit isolated.  Overall, the hotel has potential thanks to its space and setting, but the lack of attention to detail and guest experience really holds it back."
