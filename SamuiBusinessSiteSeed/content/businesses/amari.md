## Top Reviews
> "The staff were genuinely friendly throughout my stay, and the breakfast was actually quite good. The pillows were also some of the most comfortable I’ve had in a hotel for a long time.  That being said, the room itself had several issues that I simply wouldn’t expect from a 5-star hotel, especially at around 7,500 THB for two nights.  I was staying in a ground floor room, and privacy was far from ideal. People were constantly walking past and could easily look into the room, which made it difficult to feel comfortable.  The balcony was full of mosquitoes, and I also found cockroaches inside the room. The hotel should really consider carrying out a proper pest control treatment, as this is not something guests should be dealing with.  The biggest issue for me was the air conditioning. The automatic mode kept switching on and off throughout the night, and every time it did, it was loud enough to wake me up. I barely slept during my first night because of it.  Another thing that stood out was the bathroom. The bum gun next to the toilet had almost no water pressure at all. This is not a hidden defect or something difficult to notice. Anyone cleaning the bathroom on a daily basis should immediately realise that it is barely working. It made me wonder how regularly rooms are being checked before guests arrive.  Overall, the staff were great and the breakfast was enjoyable, but the room quality, maintenance issues, and pest problems fell well below what I would expect from a hotel advertising itself as a 5-star property."

The resort as a whole exceeded all expectations I had. Also had personalised hand written welcome cards, fruit and cute welcome messages written on the mirror.  The pool view suites are quite peaceful and though they are further away from the lobby, beach and restaurant, the walk is a lovely stroll. Plus you can often get a friendly staff member to drive you back on a golf cart.  Location is great. It's in a part of Chaweng that is very close to, but not completely surrounded by all the liveliness and activity that Chaweng provides. So if you want a peaceful beach or poolside holiday one day, and want a busy night of eating, drinking and dancing the next, you can easily have both.  Breakfast buffet was incredible. There's Thai and Indian sections, but also a huge section for stuff like, bacon, eggs, fruit, cereal, pancakes etc. Basically anything you associate with breakfast you will find. Cocktails were lovely. Be sure to follow happy hour around, it's at different times at different pools.  I could say a lot more but would be here a while.
