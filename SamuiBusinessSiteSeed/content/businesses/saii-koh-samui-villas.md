## Top Reviews
> "Koh Samui Saii Villas  is one of those places where you can clearly see the potential, which is why some parts of the stay ended up being frustrating. The staff were honestly exceptional from start to finish — warm, friendly, and always trying to help. It genuinely felt like they cared about guests and wanted the resort to improve. The food and beverage manager especially stood out because he seemed personally invested in making everyone’s stay as good as possible, and that level of effort deserves recognition.  The villas themselves are spacious and the resort has a peaceful, private atmosphere, but it still lacks the refined touches you expect from a luxury villa resort. We upgraded to a larger pool villa expecting complete privacy and a polished five-star feel, but there were several things that took away from that experience. A generator sat directly in front of our pool and garden area, and another villa overlooked part of the outdoor space. It may sound minor, but when you’re paying for luxury and exclusivity, these details really matter.  Maintenance and presentation also need improving. Little things kept standing out, such as lawns being cut around the sunbeds rather than underneath them, roofs looking dirty, and our pool turning green one morning. None of these issues were disastrous on their own, but together they slowly removed that premium feeling the resort is aiming for.  The beachfront was another missed opportunity. The scenery is beautiful, but the beach itself could be cleaned and maintained better. The biggest issue was access to the sea — the shoreline is so rocky that getting into the water is extremely difficult. A simple cleared pathway or small jetty into the sea would transform the experience and make the beachfront far more usable for guests.  The biggest let-down, however, was the food. One evening I ordered prawns that were clearly off and had to send them back, which completely put me off seafood for the rest of the holiday. The lamb chops and steak were frozen, breakfast items often seemed reused from previous days, and the overall quality simply didn’t match the prices being charged. We regularly found ourselves leaving the resort for dinner because nearby restaurants offered much fresher and better food for a fraction of the price. The wine list was also surprisingly limited for a resort positioning itself as luxury.  What makes this review difficult is that the staff really did try their best throughout our stay. Nothing felt intentionally careless — it felt more like a resort that hasn’t quite perfected the details yet. With better quality control, fresher food, improved maintenance, and some investment into the beachfront, this place could become genuinely special because the people working there clearly care deeply about making it succeed."
